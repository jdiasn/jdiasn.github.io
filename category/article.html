Wien's law step by step

Qua 11 maio 2016

The Wein's displacement law is obtained from Planck's law. Hereafter, I will show how it cam be found.

\begin{equation} \label{eq1} B(T,\lambda) = \frac{2hc^{2}}{\lambda^{5}(exp(\frac{hc}{kT})-1)} \end{equation}

By simplicity, let's define \(\alpha\equiv 2hc^{2}\) and \(\gamma\equiv \frac{hc}{kT}\)

So, the equation \ref{eq1} becomes

\begin{equation} \label{eq2} B(T, \lambda) = D\lambda^{-5}(exp(\frac{B}{\lambda})-1)^{-1} \end{equation}

But what we want to find? We are interested to find in which wavelength occur the maximum emissivity. For it, we need to make \(\frac{\partial B}{\partial \lambda}=0\).

\begin{equation} \begin{split} \frac{\partial B}{\partial \lambda}=D\lambda^{-6}(exp(\frac{B}{\lambda})-1) [-5+ \\ B\lambda^{-1}(exp(\frac{B}{\lambda})-1)^{-1}exp(\frac{B}{\lambda})] =0 \end{split} \label{eq3} \end{equation}

The solution of equation \ref{eq3} is getting doing the factor inside of brackets equal to zero.

\begin{equation} -5+ B\lambda^{-1}(exp(\frac{B}{\lambda})-1)^{-1}exp(\frac{B}{\lambda}) =0 \label{eq4} \end{equation}

After to reorder all factors in \ref{eq4}, we will get the equation bellow.

\begin{equation} \lambda[\exp(\frac{B}{\lambda})(\frac{B}{\lambda}-5)+5]=0 \label{eq5} \end{equation}

Once that \(\lambda\) is different of zero, the factor inside of brackets has to be zero.

\begin{equation} exp(\frac{B}{\lambda})(\frac{B}{\lambda}-5)+5=0 \label{eq6} \end{equation}

The equation \ref{eq6} is a transcendental equation, it means that this isn't possible to solve by traditional way. By simplicity, we will define \(g\equiv \frac{B}{\lambda}\). So, the least equation becomes

\begin{equation} exp(g)(g-5)+5=0 \label{eq7} \end{equation}
